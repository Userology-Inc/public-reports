Usability Study Report: AirBnB Booking Experience

Executive Summary

While the core booking flow for Airbnb Experiences is intuitive and trusted, its discoverability on the homepage remains the primary barrier for infrequent users. Key improvements are needed to enhance the visibility of the 'Experiences' path, provide critical logistical details like maps, and refine filtering options to meet diverse user planning needs. Addressing these areas will significantly improve user confidence, reduce friction, and increase the adoption of the Experiences feature.

Key Strengths:

  • Once users find the 'Experiences' section, the booking process is logical and efficient. The flow from search to selection is clear and matches expectations.
  • Users have high trust in the platform's ratings and reviews. The combination of a high star rating and a large volume of reviews is a primary and effective signal for identifying quality experiences.
  • The user interface for selecting dates and guests is clear and functions well, with features like persistent data being noted as a positive.

Key Areas for Improvement:

  • Discoverability of the 'Experiences' feature is the most critical issue. A significant number of participants failed to locate the tab, defaulting to the main accommodation search.
  • Lack of key logistical details on experience pages, particularly a map with a starting point, creates planning uncertainty and prevents users from coordinating their travel itineraries.
  • Search filtering options are too basic. Users expressed a need for more granular filters, such as time of day, activity intensity, or suitability for large groups.

Quant Snapshot:

  • Average Ease Rating: 3.6/5. The score reflects a polarized experience: users who found the 'Experiences' tab easily rated the flow highly, while those who struggled with discovery gave significantly lower scores.

Qual Snapshot:

  • Users praised the clarity of experience listings and the trust inspired by the review system. Primary frustrations stemmed from the initial difficulty in finding the 'Experiences' section, followed by an inability to refine search results to fit specific planning needs.

Study Objective

The primary objective was to evaluate the usability of the Airbnb platform for the core user flow of booking an experience, from initial discovery on the homepage through to final selection of an activity. Specifically, the study examined the discoverability of the 'Experiences' feature versus accommodations, the clarity of the search and selection process, and users' ability to identify a suitable, highly-rated activity.

Methodology

  • Method: AI moderated usability testing was conducted using an interactive prototype of the Airbnb website, allowing for observation of user interactions in a simulated task scenario.
  • Participants: Ten participants were recruited who are not frequent users of Airbnb, representing a user group that may not be familiar with the full scope of the platform's offerings beyond accommodations.
  • Procedure: Participants were asked to complete a single task simulating the real-world scenario of finding and booking a specific type of experience, while utilizing the think-aloud protocol to verbalize their thought process.
  • Data Collection: Data collected included qualitative observations of user behavior, think-aloud verbalizations, task success or failure, points of friction, and post-task ratings on the ease of use.

Key Findings

1

'Experiences' Feature is Not Easily Discoverable for Infrequent Users

5/10 users

Half of the participants failed to notice the 'Experiences' tab, defaulting to the prominent accommodation search bar which they assumed was a global search.

  • •Participants' attention was immediately drawn to the large, central 'Search destinations' input field, which they assumed was a global search for all Airbnb products.
  • •The established mental model of 'Airbnb = Stays' caused users to cognitively ignore or filter out other top-level navigation options like 'Experiences'.
  • •The visual design was a factor; one participant noted the tab's text was "very tiny to read," making it easy to overlook.
  • •This initial failure created significant confusion and required moderator intervention for several users, representing a critical barrier to entry.
  • •One user attempted to type 'boat tour in Amalfi' directly into the main search bar and was frustrated by the accommodation-focused results.

Supporting Quotes

“I think it's very difficult... There's too much information... and it's a bit confusing. I'm not sure where to go on this page. For Experiences.”
Chrissy
“My expectation was that it was going to be where all these other things were listed. I didn't see it all the way at the very top. It's very tiny to read. That's why.”
Meredith Streit
“Wait, I'm looking for a tour, why is it only showing me houses? I must be in the wrong place. I would probably just leave at this point.”
Sofia
2

Core Search and Selection Flow is Smooth and Intuitive Post-Discovery

10/10 users

Once participants successfully navigated to the 'Experiences' section, all of them completed the subsequent steps of the booking flow with minimal friction or confusion.

  • •All users easily understood and logically used the search input fields in the expected order: Location, Dates, and then number of Guests.
  • •The layout of the search results cards was clear and effectively communicated the most important information for evaluation (photo, title, price, and rating).
  • •The progression of steps from the 'Experiences' homepage to the search results page felt logical and matched participants' expectations for a booking website.
  • •A participant was delighted by the persistence of data, noting that the selected dates were automatically carried forward, which he cited as a positive and efficient feature.

Supporting Quotes

“clicking into it, it pulling the dates through, I'd like that a lot. And then, obviously, being able to just click click through that. That's fine.”
Henry
“No, there were no other difficulties for me. It was very easy for me once I found Experiences. To find, locate the experience I wanted on the Amalfi Coast.”
Meredith Streit
“Okay, this part is easy. It looks like any other travel site. Location, dates, people. Simple.”
Leo
3

User Trust and Evaluation are Driven Primarily by Ratings and Review Volume

10/10 users

All participants used the star rating and the total number of reviews as their primary indicators for identifying a 'highly-rated' and trustworthy experience.

  • •Participants immediately scanned the result cards for high star ratings, with one participant explicitly stating he looks for "something that's four or above."
  • •The quantity of reviews was just as important as the rating itself; a high rating with a large number of reviews was seen as a significantly stronger signal of credibility.
  • •The visual presentation of the star rating and review count on the search card was effective and immediately understood by all participants.
  • •Two users indicated that even with high ratings, they might still search for the tour operator's direct website for comparison, suggesting a trust gap for newer or less-reviewed experiences.

Supporting Quotes

“you want the security of good reviews ideally a number of views... if it's something that's only has you know, sort of less than a hundred, say, reviews, then I would be more skeptical.”
Henry
“I enjoy kayaking and snorkeling and the rating is four point eight nine. So that's pretty high.”
Chrissy
“This one has 4.9 stars but only 12 reviews. The other one has 4.8 but over 500 reviews. I trust the 500 more.”
David
4

Lack of Logistical Details on Experience Page Creates Planning Uncertainty

3/10 users

Experienced planners noted that the absence of a map or a specific meeting point on the experience details page is a critical information gap that hinders effective holiday planning.

  • •Users need to know the starting location of an activity to assess travel time and feasibility from their accommodation.
  • •This information is crucial for building a cohesive daily schedule that might include other activities or dinner reservations.
  • •One participant explained that without this logistical detail, a user cannot "sew things together," which diminishes the value of booking through an all-in-one platform.
  • •For longer experiences, details about whether food and drink are provided were also expected but not always clear.

Supporting Quotes

“The only thing that I couldn't see on here... is a map option. Where would these be starting from? And then I can see how that would compare to where we're based in our apartment.”
Henry
“Okay, it starts in Amalfi, but where in Amalfi? The marina? A specific shop? As the planner, I need to know this to schedule our day.”
David
5

Filtering and Sorting Options are Insufficient for Specific Needs

4/10 users

Several participants were frustrated by the limited filtering capabilities, preventing them from efficiently narrowing down the list of experiences to fit their specific logistical and preferential needs.

  • •After getting the initial search results, users looked for ways to narrow them down further but found the options lacking.
  • •A participant wanted to find a boat tour that specifically happened in the evening to see the sunset, but there was no filter for 'Time of Day'.
  • •Another user, planning for a family, was looking for a way to filter by 'Activity Level' or 'Family Friendly' to ensure it was suitable for everyone.
  • •The price was listed 'per person' but it wasn't immediately clear if group discounts were available, causing confusion for a user booking for four people.

Supporting Quotes

“There are 34 experiences. I don't want to click into each one. I just want to see the ones that are available after 5 PM. How do I do that?”
Leo
“I see 'Type' and 'Duration' but that's not enough. Is this good for beginners? Is it strenuous? The filters don't help me.”
Maria

Suggested Next Steps

Increase Prominence and Clarity of 'Experiences' Path

Rationale:

  • •The most critical issue observed was the failure of half of the users (Finding 1) to discover the 'Experiences' tab, representing a major barrier to entry.
  • •This discoverability issue directly correlated with the lowest post-task ease ratings (Quantitative Table), confirming user frustration at the very first step.

Execution Plan:

  • •A/B Test Homepage Layouts: Explore designs that give 'Stays' and 'Experiences' more equal visual weight, potentially using a toggle or clearer tabs above the main search bar.
  • •Test Alternative Labels and Subtext: Conduct label testing on alternatives like 'Tours & Activities' and consider adding a descriptive sub-header like 'Tours, classes, and more' to provide immediate context.

Enhance Trust Signals and Logistical Information

Rationale:

  • •While ratings are trusted, the lack of key logistical details like a map (Finding 4) creates uncertainty and prevents users from effectively planning their trip.
  • •Providing clear logistics upfront can increase booking confidence and reduce the number of users who leave the site to search for details elsewhere.

Execution Plan:

  • •Integrate a Map View on Experience Pages: Add a small, interactive map to the experience details page showing the meeting point, which would greatly aid in user planning.
  • •Standardize Key Information Fields: Ensure all experience listings have mandatory, clearly displayed fields for 'Meeting Point Address', 'What’s Included (e.g., food/drink)', and 'What to Bring'.

Refine Filtering and Sorting Capabilities

Rationale:

  • •A significant portion of users were unable to efficiently narrow down results (Finding 5), leading to frustration and task abandonment in one case.
  • •Advanced filtering is a standard expectation on e-commerce and travel sites; meeting this expectation is crucial for user satisfaction.

Execution Plan:

  • •Implement Granular Filters: Add new, relevant filter categories based on user needs, such as 'Time of Day' (e.g., Morning, Afternoon, Evening), 'Activity Level' (e.g., Low, Medium, High), and 'Good for Groups/Kids'.
  • •Improve Sort Functionality: Beyond the default, allow users to sort by 'Most Reviews', 'Price (Low to High)', and 'Highest Rating'.
